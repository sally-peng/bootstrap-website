In 2011, black youth were 269 percent more likely to be arrested for violating curfew laws than white youth.

It is no mystery that a vast racial bias is present in the juvenile justice system. Among those juveniles who are arrested, black juveniles are more likely to be referred to a juvenile court than are white juveniles. They are more likely to be processed (and less likely to be diverted). Among those adjudicated delinquent, they are more likely to be sent to secure confinement. Among those detained, black youth are more likely to be transferred to adult facilities. The disparities grow at almost every step.

There has been an increasing trend with the numbers of arrest for juveniles.
